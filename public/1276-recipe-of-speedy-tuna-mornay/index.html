The ingredients needed to make Tuna Mornay:
  1. Make ready 200 g Dried Pasta *Today I used 2 heaped cups Spiral Pasta
  2. Get 2 tablespoons (30 g) Butter
  3. Get 1 Onion *diced
  4. Take 1 clove Garlic *finely chopped
  5. Get 2 tablespoons Plain Flour
  6. Get 2 cups Milk *OR 1 cup Milk and 1 cup Stock
  7. Get 1/2-1 teaspoon Salt *alter the amount
  8. Make ready White Pepper
  9. Take 1/2 cup Corn Kernels *frozen is fine
  10. Take 100 g Tasty Cheese (Cheddar) *coarsely grated, save some for topping
  11. Make ready 425 g canned Tuna *drained and coarsely flaked
  12. Get 1-2 tablespoons Finely chopped Parsley
  13. Make ready Crunchy Topping
  14. Get 1/4 cup ‘Panko’ Breadcrumbs
  15. Prepare 1-2 teaspoons Olive Oil OR melted Butter
  16. Get 1/4 grated Parmesan Cheese *grated

Instructions to make Tuna Mornay:
  1. Preheat oven to 200℃. Lightly grease a large baking dish.
  2. Cook Pasta of your choice in boiling salted water, following the instruction on the package, until just tender. Drain and transfer to the sauce later.
  3. Melt Butter in a deep frying pan or a large sauce saucepan over a medium heat. Add Onion and Garlic, and cook for 2 to 3 minutes. Reduce heat to low, add Flour and stir for a minutes, and gradually add Milk (OR Milk & Stock), stirring constantly, until well combined. Cook until sauce comes to the boil.
  4. Remove sauce from heat. Add Cheese, saving some for topping, and season with Salt and Pepper. Stir until the Cheese has melted. Add Corn Kernels, drained Paste and Tuna, and mix to combine. *Note: If you used unsalted Tuna, season it with Salt before you add it to the mixture.
  5. To make Crunchy Topping, mix ‘Panko’ Breadcrumbs and Oil (OR Butter) in a small bowl, add grated Parmesan, and mix well.
  6. Spoon the pasta mixture into the baking dish. Scatter the saved Cheese and Crunchy Topping over, and bake for 20 to 25 minutes or until golden. Sprinkle finely chopped Parsley on top and serve.
