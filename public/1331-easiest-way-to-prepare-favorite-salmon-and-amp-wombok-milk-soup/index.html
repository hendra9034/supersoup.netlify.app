The ingredients needed to make Salmon & Wombok Milk Soup:
  1. Get 2 skinless Salmon Fillets *about 250g
  2. Take 2 pinches Salt
  3. Take 2 tablespoons Sake (Rice Wine)
  4. Get 1/2 Wombok *500 to 600g
  5. Make ready 2 cups Chicken Stock *OR 2 cups Water and 2 teaspoons Asian Chicken Bouillon Powder
  6. Make ready 2 cups Milk
  7. Get Salt & White Pepper
  8. Take 2 tablespoons Potato Starch OR Corn Starch *mixed with 2 tablespoons Water

Instructions to make Salmon & Wombok Milk Soup:
  1. Season Salmon Fillets with Salt and set aside while you are preparing other ingredients.
  2. In a small bowl, combine Potato Starch (OR Corn Starch) and Water. Wash Wombok and slice 2cm thick.
  3. Heat Chicken Stock in a large saucepan or pot over medium heat, add hard parts of Wombok, cover with a lid, and bring to the boil. Then add the softer parts, lower the heat and simmer.
  4. *Note: You might think 1/2 Wombok is far too much, but the volume will decrease, and 1/2 is right amount for 4 servings.
  5. Meanwhile, place Salmon Fillets in a small frying pan or a separate saucepan, sprinkle with Sake (Rice Wine), cover with a lid, and cook over low heat for 5 minutes or until cooked. Transfer to a plate, break into smaller pieces, and remove all bones. *Note: Any liquid in the pan can be added to the soup.
  6. When Wombok is cooked, add Milk, and bring back to the boil. Season with Salt & Pepper, then gradually add Potato Starch mixture, stirring, and cook until the soup thickens.
  7. Add Salmon pieces and all the liquid, and stir to combine.
