The ingredients needed to make Mochi Doughnuts:
  1. Make ready 1 cup Self-Raising Flour
  2. Take 1 cup Glutinous Rice Flour *about 100g
  3. Prepare 1/2 teaspoon Baking Powder
  4. Get 1/4 cup Caster Sugar *finer granule Sugar
  5. Prepare 1/8 teaspoon Salt
  6. Take 200 ml Milk
  7. Take Oil for frying
  8. Take Optional Icing
  9. Take 1/2 cup Icing Sugar
  10. Prepare 1 tablespoon Milk, Lemon Juice, Coffee, Matcha Tea, OR etc
  11. Take Vanilla Extract *optional

These mochi donuts are the ultimate cultural fusion dish. Chewy mochi center, crunchy, golden brown exterior, all rolled in sweet. Here we've made a slightly healthier option on doughnuts. We've used glutinous rice flour instead of wheat flour for the dough, which makes it gluten free!

Steps to make Mochi Doughnuts:
  1. Mix all ingredients to make a dough. If it is too soft, you can add a little bit extra Flour. Knead well until smooth. - *Note: I used this Glutinous Rice Flour that is available from most supermarkets in Melbourne
  2. Roll the dough into small balls, about 2cm in diameter. If you use 6 balls to form a doughnut, you would need 36 balls. It is your choice to use 8 balls for each doughnut. In that case, make 48 balls.
  3. Prepare 6 x 10cm square pieces of baking paper. Arrange the balls on each baking paper and form a ring.
  4. Heat deep Oil about 160℃, carefully place the dough with the baking paper into the Oil. As the paper will come off, carefully remove the paper, and fry the doughnuts for 4-5 minutes or until nicely golden on both sides.
  5. Transfer to a wire rack, and repeat with remaining dough.
  6. My Mochi Doughnuts are best when they are just fried and still warm.
  7. If you want to add Icing, mix Icing Sugar and 1 tablespoon liquid until smooth, dip one side of the cooled doughnut in the Icing, then leave on the wire rack until the Icing is dry to touch.
