The ingredients needed to make Wild Mushroom Ragu with Polenta and Oregano Oil:
  1. Make ready Ragu
  2. Take 375 g Portobello/field mushrooms, cleaned and sliced
  3. Make ready 150 g wild mushrooms, cleaned and left whole
  4. Get 30 g dried wild mushrooms - rehydrated in 250ml hot water
  5. Take 5 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  6. Make ready 1 large onion, finely sliced
  7. Take 1 tsp lemon thyme, leaves picked and finely chopped
  8. Prepare 1 sage leaf, chopped
  9. Get 1/4 tsp chilli flakes
  10. Make ready 1/2 tbsp tomato puree
  11. Prepare 3 small ripe tomatoes, peeled, seeded, chopped
  12. Make ready 1/2 tbsp gluten-free flour (brown rice flour or corn flour)
  13. Take 1 garlic clove, crushed
  14. Prepare 50 g flat leaf parsley, chopped
  15. Take 1 squeeze lemon juice
  16. Take sea salt and black pepper
  17. Take Polenta
  18. Prepare 300 ml vegetable stock
  19. Take 200 ml almond milk
  20. Make ready 80 g polenta (instant or traditional)
  21. Get 30 g coconut oil
  22. Make ready 20 g nutritional yeast (optional)
  23. Prepare Oregano oil
  24. Get 1 bunch fresh oregano
  25. Take 200 ml oil of choice (extra virgin, rapeseed, nut oil)
  26. Take 200 g tenderstem broccoli
Steps to make Wild Mushroom Ragu with Polenta and Oregano Oil:
  1. Start with the oregano oil to allow the flavours more chance to infuse. Pour the oil into a small saucepan. Put your oregano leaves into the oil and on a very low heat and gently heat the oil until you see a few small bubbles rise to the surface. Turn the heat off immediately and let the oil sit until ready to serve.
  2. Next focus on the ragu. To prepare the tomatoes, score the tops of them with a cross and dunk into a bowl of hot water. Leave for 30 seconds, remove and dunk into a bowl of cold water, leaving for a minute. Using the edge of the crosses, peel the skin away. Quarter the tomatoes and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Clean the mushrooms using a pastry brush, keeping the Portobello mushrooms separate.
  4. In a wide frying pan, heat 2 tbsp olive oil over a medium-high heat. Saute the onion until brown and softened. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  5. In the same pan, heat another tbsp olive oil and cook the Portobello mushrooms until golden in colour. Lower the heat, add the sage, thyme, chilli flakes, tomato puree, tomatoes, rehydrated wild mushrooms (keeping the liquid), salt and pepper, and give everything a stir, cooking for a couple of minutes to allow the flavours to infuse.
  6. Add the flour by sprinkling evenly across the pan, stirring for a few minutes to evenly incorporate and cook the flour. Add the onions back in.
  7. Pour in half the dried mushroom liquid and keep stirring to encourage the flour to thicken the liquid evenly. Once thickened, gradually add the remaining mushroom liquid in and cook for two minutes until the sauce has a gravy-like consistency. Add water to thin the sauce if desired.
  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning, it may need a squeeze of lemon juice. The ragu can now be cooled for re-heating later or freezing. However, hold it in the pan and make the polenta.
  9. Bring the stock and cashew milk to the boil in a saucepan. Slowly stir in the polenta, then reduce the heat to the minimum and cook, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon. The polenta is ready when it leaves the sides of the pan but is still runny. If you are using instant polenta this shouldn't take more than five minutes; with traditional polenta it could take up to 50 minutes (if it seems to dry out,add some more stock or water but just enough to keep it at a thick porridge consistency).
  10. Once cooked, add the coconut oil and nutritional yeast (if you are using it) and generously season (polenta takes a lot of salt and pepper). Cover with a lid and sit to one side, ready to heat up before serving.
  11. Just before serving, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a frying pan on moderate heat and add the wild mushrooms. Season and saute for two minutes until they begin to colour. Add the garlic and parsley, cook for one minute, stirring. Add the mushrooms to the sauce.
  12. Whilst these mushrooms are cooking, trim any thin stalk ends off the tenderstem broccoli. Bring water in a small saucepan to boil, place a steamer on top and steam the tenderstem for five minutes until tender but still retaining a bite.
  13. Time to serve. If the polenta has cooled slightly, re-heat and in a warm serving bowl/plate spread a smooth generous layer over the bottom of the plate. Lay across the tenderstem coating with the mushroom ragu and drizzle over the oregano oil.
