The ingredients needed to make Omurice:
  1. Get For the Rice
  2. Get 1 cup cooked rice
  3. Take 2 chicken fillets diced
  4. Get 1 onion finely chopped
  5. Get 1/2 pepper diced
  6. Prepare 2 tbsp tomato ketchup
  7. Make ready 1 knob butter (optional)
  8. Get 2 tbsp or a good glug of soy sauce
  9. Prepare Extra squirt of ketchup to dress it!
  10. Make ready For the Omelette
  11. Take 4 eggs
  12. Make ready Dash milk
  13. Prepare Salt
  14. Prepare Spray oil

Omurice, a beloved staple of Japanese home cooking, is a linguistic and literal mash-up of omelet and rice. A plain omelet cloaks ketchup-flavored fried rice, often called "chicken rice" even when it's made. Omurice is a short form of omelet and rice, the two essential ingredients of this Japanese fusion dish. The rice is usually pan-fried together with chicken and ketchup, then wrapped in a thin omelet sheet.

Steps to make Omurice:
  1. Put the butter in a frying pan, heat it on a medium setting, chop the chicken and throw that in the pan too!
  2. Whilst the chicken is cooking prepare the onion, stir the chicken then add the onion, stirring until translucent.
  3. Dice the red pepper throw it in as well and the soy sauce.
  4. Add the cooked rice.
  5. Stir the rice, traditionally in Japan you shouldn’t stir it you should flip the pan so it all mixes up! 1. Add the ketchup heat so it becomes sticky then set on a low side and set aside to make the Omlette.
  6. Crack the eggs in a bowl, add the milk and salt, with a fork whisk until smooth.
  7. Heat a smallish frying pan.
  8. Add the egg mixture.
  9. Make the omelette, they traditionally serve it in what they say is a dolphin shape.
  10. Put the rice into a bowl pressing it into a shape, then tip it upside on a plate, add the omelette and drizzle so more ketchup! Tuck in!
